Can you explain the quadratic formula?

The quadratic formula solves equations of the form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

\[x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\]

Where $a \neq 0$. The discriminant $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$ determines the nature of the roots.

What about the Pythagorean theorem?

For a right triangle, the Pythagorean theorem states:

\[a^2 + b^2 = c^2\]

Where $c$ is the hypotenuse. For example, if $a = 3$ and $b = 4$, then $c = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5$.

Show me Euler's identity.

Euler's identity is considered one of the most beautiful equations in mathematics:

\[e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0\]

It connects five fundamental constants: $e$ (Euler's number), $i$ (imaginary unit), $\pi$ (pi), $1$, and $0$.

How do I calculate the derivative of $f(x) = x^3$?

Using the power rule, the derivative is:

\[f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(x^3) = 3x^2\]

More generally, for $f(x) = x^n$, we have $f'(x) = nx^{n-1}$.

What's the integral of $\sin(x)$?

The integral of $\sin(x)$ is:

\[\int \sin(x) \, dx = -\cos(x) + C\]

Where $C$ is the constant of integration. For definite integrals: $\int_0^{\pi} \sin(x) \, dx = 2$.

Explain the binomial theorem.

The binomial theorem expands $(a + b)^n$:

\[(a + b)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} a^{n-k} b^k\]

Where $\binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$ is the binomial coefficient. For example, $(x + y)^2 = x^2 + 2xy + y^2$.

What is the matrix multiplication formula?

For matrices $A$ (size $m \times n$) and $B$ (size $n \times p$), the product $C = AB$ has elements:

\[c_{ij} = \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_{ik} b_{kj}\]

The result is an $m \times p$ matrix. Matrix multiplication is not commutative: $AB \neq BA$ in general.

Show me the definition of a limit.

The limit of $f(x)$ as $x$ approaches $a$ is $L$, written $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$, if:

\[\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0 : |x - a| < \delta \implies |f(x) - L| < \epsilon\]

This is the formal $\epsilon$-$\delta$ definition of a limit.

What's the formula for compound interest?

Compound interest is calculated as:

\[A = P\left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}\]

Where $A$ is the final amount, $P$ is principal, $r$ is annual rate, $n$ is compounding frequency, and $t$ is time in years. For continuous compounding: $A = Pe^{rt}$.
