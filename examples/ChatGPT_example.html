Soap Production (Saponification) - Chemical Equations

  1. General Reaction (Exact)
    Triglyceride + Alkali → Soap (fatty acid salts) + Glycerol

    (RCOO)3C3H5+3 NaOH  ⟶  3 RCOONa+C3H5(OH)3\mathrm{(RCOO)_3C_3H_5 + 3\,NaOH \;\longrightarrow\; 3\,RCOONa + C_3H_5(OH)_3}(RCOO)3​C3​H5​+3NaOH⟶3RCOONa+C3​H5​(OH)3​

    Where:

    • RCOO\mathrm{RCOO}RCOO = fatty acid residue (e.g., palmitate, stearate, oleate)

    • RCOONa\mathrm{RCOONa}RCOONa = sodium soap

    • C3H5(OH)3\mathrm{C_3H_5(OH)_3}C3​H5​(OH)3​ = glycerol

  3. Potassium Soap Variant (Exact)

    (RCOO)3C3H5+3 KOH  ⟶  3 RCOOK+C3H5(OH)3\mathrm{(RCOO)_3C_3H_5 + 3\,KOH \;\longrightarrow\; 3\,RCOOK + C_3H_5(OH)_3}(RCOO)3​C3​H5​+3KOH⟶3RCOOK+C3​H5​(OH)3​

  4. Mechanistic View (Inferred)

    • Base-catalyzed ester hydrolysis

    • Nucleophilic attack of OH−\mathrm{OH^-}OH− on ester carbonyl

    • Irreversible due to formation of fatty acid salt
